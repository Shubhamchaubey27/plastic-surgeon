What is a Brow Lift Surgery?

Brow lift surgery, also known as a forehead lift, addresses and surgically fixes the drooping eyebrows and wrinkles on the forehead to restore a youthful, refreshed look.

Understanding The Different Brow Lift Techniques

Tired of drooping eyebrows and forehead wrinkles? Brow lift surgery can address these concerns and restore a youthful appearance. There are two main types of brow lift techniques, each with its own advantages:

Endoscopic Brow Lift: Minimally Invasive Rejuvenation Technique. This advanced technique utilizes a tiny camera (endoscope) to visualize and adjust the forehead tissues through small incisions hidden within the hairline. Ideal for mild to moderate drooping brows, endoscopic lifts offer several benefits:

  • Faster Recovery: Smaller incisions translate to quicker healing and less downtime.
  • Minimal Scarring: Strategically placed incisions ensure virtually invisible scars.
  • Suitable for mild to moderate brow drooping and forehead wrinkles.
  • Natural-Looking Results: Dr. Garg’s expertise delivers a refreshed appearance without appearing “overdone.”

Benefits of Brow Lift Surgery

  • Smooth and Youthful Upper Face
  • Enhanced Attractiveness
  • Enhanced Confidence and Self-esteem
  • Restored a Balanced and Revitalized Face

Trichophytic Brow Lift: Ideal for patients with high hairlines. This open technique involves an incision hidden within the hairline to remove excess forehead skin and elevate the brows. Trichophytic lifts are ideal for patients with a high hairline who desire a brow lift without altering their hairline further. However, this technique is less common due to:

  • Limited Application: It’s not suitable for everyone and may not address deeper wrinkles.

Coronal Brow Lift: Addressing More Significant eyebrows drooping. This traditional open technique involves a single incision across the top of the forehead, from ear to ear. Coronal lifts are suitable for significant brow drooping or patients with a high hairline. However, due to the larger incision, this method might cause:

  • Chronic Numbness: Numbness around the incision site can occur and may take time to resolve.
  • Longer Recovery: Healing time is typically longer compared to endoscopic lifts.

Non-Surgical Brow Lift with Neurotoxins (Botox):Temporary Solution Botox injections relax the muscles that pull the brows downward, resulting in a slight brow lift. This non-surgical option offers:

  • Minimal Downtime: No recovery period.
  • Subtle Results: Effects are temporary and require regular touch-up injections (typically every 2-3 months).
